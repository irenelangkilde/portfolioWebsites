About

My work sits at the intersection of laser hardware, RF systems, simulation, and real lab validation. At BYU’s Laser Ablation Lab, I helped develop a laser ablation tool for fabricating RF metal mesh filters and microscopic flexible cables. At Raytheon, I tested and troubleshot RF circuit cards, wrote test code, and correlated simulation results to measured hardware behavior. I’m especially interested in roles where careful technical execution, diagnostic thinking, and hardware reliability matter.
