About

I spent two summers at Raytheon testing RF circuit cards, writing Python test code, running EME/EMI tests, and correlating simulation output to hardware measurements on network analyzers and spectrum analyzers. That hands-on validation workflow — moving between bench instruments and simulation tools to diagnose why hardware behaves differently from the model — is exactly the work I want to keep doing in a field service role.

My graduate research in RF filter design adds the analytical depth to reason about root causes, not just symptoms. I come in already fluent in the tools and terminology a field service role demands.

I am open to full-time field service or RF hardware engineering roles supporting RF, microwave, or defense systems — open to the Northwest region and travel requirements typical of field service work.
