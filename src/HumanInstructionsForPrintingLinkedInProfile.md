#HumanInstructionsForPrintingLinkedInProfile.md

Desktop (LinkedIn website)

* Open profile: Sign in and go to your profile (click your photo → View profile).
* More menu: Click the More button (near your headline/photo).
* Save as PDF: Choose Save to PDF (or Save profile → Download PDF) — the browser will download a PDF of your public profile.
* If no Save option: Use the browser print dialog and save as PDF: press Cmd+P (macOS) or Ctrl+P (Windows) → set Destination to Save as PDF → Save.

Mobile (LinkedIn app — iOS / Android)

* Open profile: Sign in, tap your profile photo → View Profile.
* More / ⋯ menu: Tap the three-dot menu (top-right).
* Save / Share: Tap Share profile via or Save to PDF (if shown). If the app doesn't offer PDF, choose Share profile via → email to yourself or open in browser, then use the device print-to-PDF feature.


Alternate: export profile data (structured)

* Settings → Data privacy: On desktop go to Me → Settings & Privacy → Data privacy → Get a copy of your data to request downloads (not a PDF resume but profile data/CSV).

Troubleshooting & tips

* Logged-in access required: You must be signed in and viewing your own profile.
* Layout differences: LinkedIn UI changes; look under More, Share, or the three-dot menu.
* Include/reduce sections: Use print options (Layout, Scale, Background graphics) to control what appears in the PDF.
* Privacy: Review content before sharing the PDF.
