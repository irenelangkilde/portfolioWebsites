Tips

  1. Don't aim for perfection and completeness on the first iteration. Get a draft as quickly as possible and then refine it.
  2. Show, don’t just tell: add proof (projects, metrics, links, screenshots, outcomes).
  3. Lead with a 1-line headline that states your target role + specialty + value.
  4. Quantify impact wherever possible (%, $, time saved, volume, accuracy, ratings).
  5. Write for skimmers: short sections, strong headings, bullets, plenty of whitespace.
  6. Tailor to the job ad: mirror keywords (truthfully) and map your evidence to their requirements.
  7. Make contact frictionless: email + LinkedIn visible, plus a clear call-to-action.

    Custom advice (generated)

    How to download a PDF of your LinkedIn profile A clean “resume-style” export you can upload here.

    On Desktop (recommended)

    1. Open your LinkedIn profile page (logged in).
    2. Click the More button near your profile header (often next to “Open to” / “Add profile section”).
    3. Select Save to PDF.
    4. Upload that PDF in the “Upload Files” section of this form.

    If you don’t see “Save to PDF”

    • Try a different browser, or disable aggressive pop-up blockers.
    • Ensure you’re viewing your profile page (not “public profile settings”).
    • LinkedIn changes UI labels occasionally—look for “Download” / “Export” profile actions.

    Pro tip

    • Also copy/paste your About, Experience, and Projects sections into the “Text Entries” area—this makes it easier to reuse content selectively.
